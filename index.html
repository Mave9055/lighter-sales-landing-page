From Training to Combat: Arthur P. Lancy's Verified Service Record

This lighter tells a complete story - from pioneering aviation training to combat service in Vietnam. In 1965, WOC Arthur P. Lancy Jr. was part of the first class to train on the revolutionary Hughes TH-55A helicopter at Fort Wolters. This lighter was presented to commemorate that achievement. But the story didn't end there.

"WOC ARTHUR P. LANCY JR.
CLASS 65-9WA
FORT WOLTERS, TEXAS"

By April 1967, Lancy had deployed to Vietnam and was flying combat missions with Company B, 25th Aviation Battalion. His service earned him the Air Medal - official recognition of his meritorious achievement while participating in aerial flight in a combat zone.

This lighter represents both the beginning and the continuation of a combat aviator's career. It connects a transformative moment in military aviation training with the reality of combat service in Vietnam. Few artifacts can document such a complete narrative - from training through decoration.
