Overview

This document provides an archival-style overview of a 1965 U.S. Army Aviation presentation lighter associated with the primary helicopter training class 65‑9WA at Fort Wolters, Texas. It is designed as a combined web and print dossier suitable for collectors, curators, and institutions.

The structure follows museum-adjacent practice: contextual overview, service and training timeline, physical description, and a formal certificate of provenance for recordkeeping and insurance purposes.

Service & Training Timeline

Early 1965 – Fort Wolters, Texas

Class 65‑9WA begins primary helicopter training on the Hughes TH‑55A Osage airframe at the U.S. Army Primary Helicopter School at Fort Wolters, Texas.

Mid 1965 – Presentation of Lighter

Presentation lighter engraved for WOC Arthur P. Lancy Jr. recognizing his participation in the first graduating class using the Hughes TH‑55A helicopter at Fort Wolters.

Vietnam War Era – Aviation Service Context

The training pipeline at Fort Wolters feeds directly into U.S. Army rotary‑wing operations in the Vietnam theater, where the TH‑55A serves as a primary trainer for pilots transitioning to combat aircraft.

Subsequent Decades – Private Custody

The lighter remains in private custody, retaining period engraving, maker marks, and surface characteristics consistent with mid‑1960s U.S.‑made presentation lighters.

Artifact Details

Physical Description

Rectangular metal petrol lighter, brushed finish, engraved with “Class 65‑9WA” and Hughes “World Leader” insignia on obverse, and dedication text to WOC Arthur P. Lancy Jr. on reverse.

Maker & Markings

Marked “PARK – Made in U.S.A.” at the base, consistent with period American‑manufactured lighters used for military and corporate presentation pieces.

Condition Notes

Engraving remains legible with minimal loss, finish exhibits age‑appropriate wear, and structural components present intact unless otherwise noted in an attached condition report.
