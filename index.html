What the Air Medal Means

The Air Medal is awarded to any person who, while serving in any capacity with the Armed Forces, distinguishes themselves by meritorious achievement while participating in aerial flight. In Vietnam, this typically meant flying combat missions under hostile fire, conducting medical evacuations under fire, or performing aerial reconnaissance in contested airspace. WO Lancy earned this decoration for his actions as an Army aviator with the 25th Aviation Battalion.

Complete Service Timeline

1965

Fort Wolters Training - Class 65-9WA

Warrant Officer Candidate Arthur P. Lancy Jr. graduates from U.S. Army Primary Helicopter Training School as part of Class 65-9WA - the first class to train exclusively on the Hughes TH-55A "Osage" helicopter. This lighter was presented to commemorate his graduation from this historic first class.

1965-1966

Advanced Training & Commissioning

After completing primary training at Fort Wolters, Lancy would have proceeded to Fort Rucker, Alabama for advanced helicopter training. Upon completion, he was commissioned as a Warrant Officer and received orders for Vietnam.

1966-1967

Combat Deployment - 25th Aviation Battalion

WO Arthur P. Lancy deployed to Vietnam and served with Company B, 25th Aviation Battalion. The 25th Aviation Battalion provided aviation support to the 25th Infantry Division, conducting combat assault operations, medical evacuations, resupply missions, and reconnaissance flights throughout III Corps Tactical Zone.

April 1967

Air Medal Decoration

Officially documented in Tropic Lightning News, April 3, 1967: "WO Arthur P. Lancy, Co. B, 25th Avn. Bn." awarded the Air Medal for meritorious achievement while participating in aerial flight.
