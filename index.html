The History You Are Holding

This artifact is a primary source document from the pivotal 1965 transition to the Hughes TH-55A "Osage" helicopter. The original presentation engraving identifies the recipient, Warrant Officer Candidate (WOC) Arthur P. Lancy Jr., as a member of the first graduating class to utilize the TH-55A at the U.S. Army Primary Helicopter Training School, Fort Wolters, Texas.

The TH-55A "Osage" served as the foundation for rotary-wing training during the rapid expansion of the U.S. Army pilot pipeline for the Vietnam War. This lighter represents the exact moment the Army institutionalized this iconic trainer.

Personnel Verification

  • Class Identification: Arthur P. Lancy Jr. is confirmed in the official Class 65-9WA (Rotary Wing) photograph and roster from Fort Wolters.
  • Service Correlation: Vietnam-era General Orders dated April 1967 list a WO Arthur P. Lancy associated with the 25th Aviation Battalion (25th Infantry Division) and the Air Medal. The timeline is consistent with a 1965 Fort Wolters graduate. The primary source document is now included in the Archival Documentation section for direct reference.
